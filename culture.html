CULTURAL IMPACT

Since its release in 2015, Undertale has sold millions of copies and made a lasting impact on the indie video game scene. Created primarily by Toby Fox, the game quickly developed a dedicated fanbase with its unique humor and message.

CHOOSING MERCY

Unlike most video games, Undertale gives players the option to fight enemies or peacefully settle issues with them. Toby Fox has cited Shin Megami Tensei, another video game with a similar conflict resolution style, as an inspiration. The game’s branching timelines show the player the impact of their actions and the value of choosing peace over war. Undertale's special mechanics help communicate a theme of non-violence rarely seen in video games, which often have the goal of beating enemies without regard for their fates.



ONLINE COMMUNITY

Undertale has built its reputation as a classic among video game lovers. Its meaningful plot and moving scenes are remembered and cherished by players all over the world. Undertale has appeared in countless memes – one of its songs, MEGALOVANIA, is particularly notorious, and the character Sans is instantly recognizable to players and non-players alike. The game has grown a mostly young online fanbase that, even years after its release, continues to celebrate it.


UNDERTALE'S LEGACY

Undertale holds a special place in the hearts of many and has changed how millions view video games. Although video games are often thought of as purely for entertainment, Undertale’s format as a role-player game where the player controls their destiny has allowed it to connect more deeply with players and make its message for peace even more powerful. In 2018, Toby Fox followed up with the release of Deltarune, a game set in an alternate universe to that of Undertale that features some of its characters. Deltarune also makes commentary on the use of violence, except it focuses on the acceptable use of violence in the face of oppression.
